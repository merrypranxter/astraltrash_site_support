Tensor
Tantrum

He is not an image with a jitter filter slapped on his ass. Every pixel, eye, tooth, horn, glyph, RGB ghost, and escaping chunk is drawn by JavaScript. Click him. Click near him. Hover him. Scroll violently. Poke the controls and observe the tiny bastard adapting.

↓ Scroll = pressure, not distance ↓

Chroma mites eat momentum.

Scrolling feeds his chromatic-aberration species. Fast scrolling makes him surf the signal; using the wheel while the pointer is directly over him produces a stronger mutation.

Clicks have blast radii.

A direct hit causes a full tantrum. A nearby hit startles him away from the impact. A far click on a control still reaches the control normally, while Tensor chooses a reaction from its label. His overlay uses pointer-events: none, so he cannot smother the page.

This is a real anchor

The glitches remember being fed.

Three bounded error species compete inside him: chroma mites, bit fleas, and syntax spores. Scroll feeds chroma. Buttons feed bits or syntax. Direct clicks feed the whole ecosystem. Their current populations persist only in this browser tab via sessionStorage.
